## Key Demo Tips

- **Flow**: Never stop moving between screens. Rehearse transitions until seamless.
- **Story anchor**: Every section connects back to Wireless. Judges remember stories, not features.
- **Contrast**: Always contrast with TradFi ("Allianz takes 30 days, we take 3 seconds").
- **Numbers**: Real market numbers only ($6.5B, 13.6% CAGR, 71% demographic). Judges validate.
- **Language for non-crypto judges**: Never say "PDA," "cNFT," "bonding curve" unprompted. Say "dynamic pricing," "digital card," "instant payout." Save technical terms for Q&A if asked.
- **The gold card moment**: This sells the product. Rehearse the pause. Let anticipation build.
- **The payout moment**: Point at the number. Hold your finger there for 3 full seconds.

## Backup Q&A

**"Isn't this gambling?"**

No. Three key differences. First, Shield Cards have insurance utility: they pay out on verifiable real-world events that cause real financial loss, unlike cosmetic loot boxes which deliver zero financial protection. Second, Event Shields require ticket ownership, establishing insurable interest, the legal foundation separating insurance from wagering. Third, the PMC (2021, PMCID: PMC7882574) study on loot box mechanics studied cosmetic rewards. Our cards carry real parametric insurance value, closer to buying travel insurance than buying a lottery ticket.

**"What about loot box regulations?"**

Belgium and the Netherlands have banned certain loot box mechanics, specifically cosmetic-only randomized purchases in games. CrowdShield's Shield Cards differ in a critical way: every card carries real-world insurance utility with a defined parametric trigger and payout. This resembles randomized insurance product bundles more than cosmetic gacha. We track regulatory developments closely and structure cards to maintain utility-bearing classification. That said, jurisdiction-specific legal review will precede each market launch.

**"How do you set trigger probabilities?"**

Oracle data combined with actuarial modeling. Wildfire triggers use historical satellite data (NASA FIRMS) + seasonal probability models. Flight disruption uses Eurocontrol historical ATFM data. Festival cancellation uses base rates from industry data (40+ cancellations across ~800 major festivals = ~5% base rate). Probabilities inform pack EV design, rarity weighting, and bonding curve parameterization. Not guessing. Every trigger has a data-backed probability estimate with defined confidence intervals.

**"What about LP risk?"**

Honest answer: if cancellation rates exceed ~5% across covered events, LP returns go negative. At normal rates (~3%), LPs earn ~48% APY from premiums + idle DeFi yield. Mitigation: bonding curve ensures late cover purchases contribute more to the pool. 80% pool utilization cap maintains a 20% solvency buffer at all times. Seasonal pool resets limit correlation risk across event categories. We disclose risk metrics to LPs upfront with historical backtesting. This mirrors insurance underwriting: profitable on average, lossy in tail events.

**"How do you resolve disputes?"**

We don't. Binary resolution only. Event cancelled: yes or no. Multi-source oracle (minimum 2-of-3 independent sources) confirms. Payout triggers automatically. No committees, no governance votes, no appeals process. Deterministic resolution removes the #1 source of insurance customer frustration: the claims process.

**"What's your moat?"**

Network effects on three axes. More fans = better controversy scoring data = more accurate pricing. More accurate pricing = better LP yields = more capital. More capital = cheaper covers = more fans. Plus Cover NFT and Shield Card secondary markets create liquidity lock-in: once your cards have trading history and portfolio value, switching costs become real.

**"How do you research the cards?"**

Each card is backed by public data. Wildfire probabilities from EFFIS 10-year satellite records. Earthquake odds from USGS catalog. Hurricane stats from NOAA historical data. Flight disruption from Eurocontrol ATFM records. We don't guess. We read the data, compute historical trigger frequencies, and set odds accordingly. Every probability estimate has a named source and a defined methodology.

**"Why not just do this off-chain?"**

Three reasons. (1) Transparent pool solvency, anyone can verify on-chain. (2) Composable Cover NFTs, secondary market liquidity without building a proprietary exchange. (3) Provably fair pack randomness via Switchboard VRF, critical for regulatory defensibility of the pack mechanic.
